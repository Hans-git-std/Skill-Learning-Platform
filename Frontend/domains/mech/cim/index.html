CNC Interpolation Algorithms

CNC controllers use interpolation to move tools along smooth paths using discrete stepper motor pulses. Digital Differential Analyzer (DDA) is a classic line algorithm.

DDA Algorithm:

$$ \Delta = \max(|dx|, |dy|) $$ $$ x_{new} = x_{old} + \frac{dx}{\Delta}, \quad y_{new} = y_{old} + \frac{dy}{\Delta} $$

Robotic Kinematics

Industrial robotic arms require Inverse Kinematics (IK) to calculate joint angles $(\theta_1, \theta_2)$ to reach a specific $(x, y)$ coordinate.

2R Planar Arm IK:

$$ \cos(\theta_2) = \frac{x^2 + y^2 - L_1^2 - L_2^2}{2 L_1 L_2} $$

AGV Path Planning

Automated Guided Vehicles (AGVs) navigate factory floors avoiding obstacles. The A* (A-star) algorithm is a common pathfinding technique.

A* Heuristic Cost:

$$ f(n) = g(n) + h(n) $$

Where $g(n)$ is the cost from start to node $n$, and $h(n)$ is the estimated cost (Manhattan distance) to the goal. Add obstacles and watch the AGV recalculate.

CH 09

PID Control for Servo Drives

Precise CNC axis positioning relies on closed-loop PID (Proportional-Integral-Derivative) controllers to minimize position error $e(t)$.

PID Control Law:

$$ u(t) = K_p e(t) + K_i \int e(t) dt + K_d \frac{de}{dt} $$

Tune the gains. Too much $K_p$ causes oscillation. $K_d$ dampens the response. $K_i$ eliminates steady-state error.
